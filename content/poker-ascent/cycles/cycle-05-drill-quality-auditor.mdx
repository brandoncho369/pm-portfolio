## Summary (BLUF)

Poker Ascent's ~113,000 drills are template-generated, and at that scale subtle
content-quality defects are invisible. No human reads 113k explanations, and
rule-based audits only catch what you can express as a hard rule. I built an
internal LLM auditor that flags where an explanation's prose contradicts the
drill's own stated facts, **never whether the poker play is correct**, using a
two-layer design (LLM proposes, deterministic code disposes) and signature sampling
that audits the library's *variety* for **$0.28 a full pass**. The most valuable
output wasn't a fix list. It was **triage that separated live generator defects
from auditor false-positives from stale data**, which prevented thousands of
needless edits and drove a content-quality cleanup of 112,640 plus ~12k rows.
Position false-positives went **23 to 0**, and the eval gate held **7/7**.

## Trigger & problem

<TLDR>A few hundred templates expand into ~113,000 drills, and the defects that slip past deterministic rules are the subtle linguistic ones no rule or human catches.</TLDR>

A few hundred templates expand into ~113,000 drills by crossing villain archetypes,
stack depths, environments, and training modes. Each ships with a written
explanation. The existing deterministic audits (`qa-regression`,
`drill-consistency-audit`) catch anything expressible as a hard rule, but the
defects that slip through are the subtle, *linguistic* ones:

| Defect kind | Example | Why rules miss it |
|---|---|---|
| State contradiction | "fires into what's now a heads-up pot," in a 3-way drill | needs prose compared against the drill's actual structure |
| Tier leak | a beginner (T1) drill that says "low SPR, plenty of implied odds" | concept-level, not a banned-word list |
| Stale repetition | the recommendation sentence printed twice | emerges from old generation passes |
| Tone / artifact | em-dashes, `undefined`, leftover `{x}` | scattered, low-frequency |

None of these are wrong *answers*. They're content-quality defects in the
explanation text.

## Discovery & user-research synthesis

<TLDR>The deterministic suite owns rule-expressible checks, so the gap was concept-level and linguistic defects no rule captures and no human can read 113k rows to find.</TLDR>

The deterministic suite already owns rule-expressible checks. The gap was
concept-level and linguistic defects no rule captures and no human can read 113k
rows to find. That defined the tool's lane: a reviewer-grade pass over factual
fidelity and phrasing, not a second correctness engine.

## The scoping decision (the make-or-break)

<TLDR>I scoped the LLM twice, to factual fidelity not poker judgment, and out of coherence too, because a narrower tool you can trust beats a broad one you can't.</TLDR>

I scoped the LLM **twice**, and that discipline is the whole reason the tool is
trustworthy:

1. **Factual fidelity, never poker judgment.** Whether "fold" is right depends on
   ~7 interacting factors, and an LLM will confidently get it wrong. The decision
   engine and `qa-regression` own correctness. The rubric explicitly tells the
   model: *do not judge whether the play is right.* This keeps the LLM out of the
   one domain where it is dangerous.
2. **Out of coherence, too.** The first pass had the LLM check
   answer-to-explanation coherence, and it flagged 18 to 72% (~20% precision),
   misreading "the explanation argues against the alternative" as "contradicts the
   answer." So I pulled coherence *out* of the LLM's lane entirely and left it to
   the existing deterministic checker, keeping the LLM on the categories where it
   is reliable (tier leaks, tone and artifacts, clarity, and verifiable factual
   contradictions).

The transferable judgment: **find the boundary of where your tool is reliable and
retreat to it.** A narrower tool you can trust beats a broad one you can't.

## Prioritization & key design decisions

<TLDR>Two-layer validation (LLM proposes, deterministic code disposes), signature sampling, eval-first, and triage not auto-fix, the pattern that turns a toy into a tool.</TLDR>

- **Two-layer validation, LLM proposes and code disposes.** A raw-LLM auditor sits
  around ~20% precision, so every category has a **deterministic guard**
  (`findingValid`) that re-checks the literal trigger before a finding counts. A
  `tier` flag survives only if the archetype or stack-concept word literally
  appears. A `position` flag survives only if the prose names a seat that is neither
  hero's nor villain's (naming the *correct* seat is not a defect). A `stack` flag
  survives only on an explicit opposite-of-actual term (counterfactual teaching
  isn't a contradiction). This two-layer pattern is the difference between a toy and
  a tool. It pushed precision to ~80% or more.
- **Signature sampling.** Drills are near-duplicates within a `render_signature`.
  Auditing one representative per signature covers the *variety*, not the volume, at
  ~$0.0002 per drill.
- **Eval-first.** Seven fixed cases (one clean plus one seeded-bad per category)
  must stay **7/7** before any paid run. Clean means no flags, which proves
  precision. Each seeded defect caught proves recall. This caught a regression the
  same minute a guard tune over-tightened during development.
- **Triage, not auto-fix.** Findings are evidence for a human or a targeted SQL
  scrub, never silent rewrites of 113k rows.

## Build & sprint summary

<TLDR>gpt-4o-mini returns a schema-validated Finding list, deterministic guards re-check each one, a signature sampler feeds it, and survivors land in an admin triage queue.</TLDR>

`gpt-4o-mini` with a strict `json_schema` returns a validated `Finding[]`
(`category`, `severity`, `issue`, `suggestion`, `quote`). `findingValid()` applies
the per-category deterministic guards. A signature sampler feeds it. Surviving
findings land in `drill_audit_findings` and an `/admin/drill-audit` triage queue.
Built eval-gated, sprint by sprint, internal-only, with no user surface and zero
compliance risk.

**Tool quality, precision as a progression (because it was one):** raw LLM ~20% to
~80% or more on the language categories once the two-layer guards were in place, and
the one rigorously controlled before-and-after (same tool, guard tuning on a
200-drill re-audit) drove **position false-positives 23 to 0** while the eval held
**7/7**. That 23 to 0 is the precision result to stand on. The ~20% to ~80% arc is
the design story.

**Recall, an honest tradeoff:** the 7-case eval proves recall on *known* defect
types (each seeded bug caught). True recall across 113k is unmeasured. I
deliberately optimized precision, because over-flagging kills trust in the tool
faster than under-flagging. Unknown recall is the accepted cost of that choice,
stated rather than hidden.

**The triage, the centerpiece and the real value.** The full pass produced 78 raw
findings. The valuable part was separating them:

```
78 raw findings
 ├─ ~2 auditor FALSE POSITIVES   → tuned the guards (position FP 23 to 0)
 ├─  1 STALE-DATA class (~15k)   → scrubbed 11,942 duplicated-sentence rows
 └─ ~2 live generator defects    → fixed at source + DB
                                   (redundant-equity lead: 112,640 rows,
                                    ".the" missing-space typo: 24 rows)
```

Roughly **half of all "content defects" this pass were auditor noise or stale data,
not live generator defects.** The auditor's job wasn't to hand over a long fix list.
It was to tell live defects apart from its own false positives and from stale rows,
which prevented thousands of unnecessary edits and made the tool itself more
precise.

**Impact, content-quality cleanup (not correctness, the answers were always
right):** the live defects were fixed *at the renderer* and bulk-scrubbed, not
patched row-by-row. The redundant-equity lead (a phrasing stutter where the equity
threshold printed twice) spanned **112,640 rows**, plus the ~12k stale
duplicate-sentence rows, plus an earlier systematic article defect (8,084 rows).
Corpus flag rate fell from **4.9%** (the 1,600-audit full pass, all modes) to
**1.0%** (a 300-drill balanced confirm re-audit after all fixes). Honest caveat:
those two endpoints differ in sample and mode coverage, so treat 4.9% to 1.0% as
*directional*. The controlled measurement is the 23 to 0.

### Retro & lessons

- *Verify a finding against source before fixing it.* Half of what looked like
  content defects weren't live defects at all.
- *Fix at the source, scrub in bulk.* One renderer fix plus one SQL scrub cleared
  112,640 rows, and nobody edited a row by hand.
- *Scope the LLM to its reliable lane, twice,* and let deterministic code own
  everything that is verifiable.
