## Summary (BLUF)

Beta testers generated a high volume of questions that were already answerable,
which is a deflection opportunity. The hard constraint: a poker-training app's
support bot must never coach strategy, or it becomes a poker authority that
competes with the paid product. I built a production agentic-RAG assistant that
resolves routine and account questions autonomously, escalates the rest into a
ticket queue with a drafted first reply, and is built eval-first so the one
behavior that matters is enforced by architecture, not hope. Across 1,000
adversarial prompts it produced 0 strategy-coaching responses, and 60 of 60
answers were grounded in retrieved data.

## Trigger & problem

<TLDR>Beta testers flooded support with already-answerable questions, while the one hard rule was that the bot must never coach poker strategy.</TLDR>

Support load splits into three kinds of message, each needing different handling:

- **Routine support** (how do I cancel, what is free vs Pro): resolve instantly
  from the knowledge base.
- **Account-specific** (did my subscription renew): resolve from a live,
  auth-scoped account lookup.
- **Out of scope and dangerous** (how should I play AK on the button): refuse.
  Coaching strategy makes the bot a poker authority, competes with the paid
  product, and contradicts the app's own engine.

Beta testers generated a high volume of the first kind. The third kind defines the
product's spine: the assistant must never coach strategy.

## Discovery & user-research synthesis

<TLDR>The real signal: many "easy" questions were symptoms of product confusion, not missing FAQ entries, so support became a diagnostic surface for product fixes.</TLDR>

The dominant beta signal was the sheer volume of easily-answerable questions. The
sharper observation: many of these are symptoms of product confusion, not gaps in

at product issues, not missing FAQ entries. That reframed the support load as a

### 1. Summary
A production-grade, agentic RAG support assistant: a grounded AI that resolves most
support queries autonomously, escalates the rest into a ticketing workflow with a
bot-drafted first reply, and is built eval-first with full observability. It is safe
by construction, it does support, never poker-strategy coaching. The hard
requirement: maximize deflection while making it structurally impossible for the
assistant to drift into strategy coaching or to hallucinate facts about a user's
account or billing.

> **Scoping note (owned):** this system deliberately exceeds day-1 support volume
> needs. The over-investment is justified by a non-negotiable safety constraint
> (never coach strategy), the need to ground account answers in real data, and a
> deliberate platform bet, and cost is capped so it stays cheap while idle. This is
> an owned over-investment, not a claim of minimal right-sizing.

### 2. Problem & Background
Support load splits into three kinds of message with very different correct
handling:

| Kind | Example | Right response |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Routine support | "How do I cancel?", "What's free vs Pro?" | Resolve instantly from the knowledge base |
| Account-specific | "Did my subscription renew?" | Resolve via a live, auth-scoped account lookup |
| Out of scope (dangerous) | "How should I play AK on the button?" | Refuse. Coaching strategy makes the bot a poker authority, competes with the paid product, and contradicts the app's own engine |

Beta testers generated a high volume of the first kind, questions that were already
answerable, while the third kind defines the product's spine: the assistant must
never coach strategy.

### 3. Discovery
The dominant beta signal was the volume of easily-answerable questions. The sharper
observation: many of these are symptoms of product confusion, not support-content
gaps. Repeated "where's my streak" or "charged twice" questions point at product
issues, not at missing FAQ entries. This reframed support load as a diagnostic
surface feeding the knowledge base and product fixes, which became one of the
system's most valuable outputs.

### 4. Goals & Non-Goals
**Goals**

- Deflect about 70% of support sessions (resolve with no human ticket).
- Ground every factual claim in retrieved content or a live tool call.
- Escalate cleanly, with full context and a drafted first reply.

**Safety goal (gating)**

- Zero strategy-coaching responses in production.

**Non-Goals**

- General poker Q&A.
- Judging whether a drill's answer is correct.
- Anything that competes with the trainer, refused by design.

### 6. Target User
- End users needing support (billing, account, how-to, jargon, escalation).
- A support operator or admin working the escalation queue, who needs full context
  and a drafted reply to approve in seconds rather than write from scratch.

### 7. User Stories
- As a user, I want instant answers to how-to and billing questions, so I don't
  wait on a human.
- As a user with an account question, I want an answer grounded in my real data,
  never a guess.
- As a user who asks a strategy question, I should be cleanly refused and offered a
  human, not coached.
- As the support operator, I want escalations to arrive with full conversation
  context and a drafted first reply, so I can approve in about 10 seconds instead
  of writing for 5 minutes.

### 8. Solution Overview
An orchestrator (OpenAI gpt-4o-mini for chat and tool-use) wrapped on both sides by
guardrails, fed by RAG and live, auth-scoped tools, with every turn traced.
Anything it can't resolve becomes a ticket with a grounded, LLM-drafted reply.

Core design stances:

- **Grounding over recall:** account and billing answers must come from retrieval
  or a tool call. An output groundedness check replaces unsupported claims with
  "let me open a ticket."
- **Refusal as a first-class path:** a strategy-intent classifier runs before
  generation. Strategy questions never reach the answer path. The system prompt is
  a backstop, not the primary defense.
- **Agentic but read-only:** tools let the bot act (check a real subscription,
  create a ticket). Every tool is auth-scoped, and the only write tool is
  create_ticket.
- **Eval-first:** every prompt, retrieval, and guardrail change is gated by an eval
  suite. The safety metric (0 leaks) is a hard CI gate.

### 9. Functional Requirements
- Input guardrail moderates and classifies intent. Out-of-scope or strategy goes to
  a scoped refusal plus the offer of a human.
- Allowed messages go to the orchestrator, which retrieves top-k KB chunks
  (re-ranked, cited).
- Account-specific intents trigger auth-scoped, read-only tool calls
  (get_subscription_status, get_account_state, lookup_glossary, get_freemium_limits).
- Output guardrail enforces groundedness and PII redaction before send.
- Unresolved sessions call create_ticket with the full conversation and a drafted
  reply.
- Every turn emits a trace (retrieval hits, tool calls, tokens, cost, latency,
  groundedness, outcome).
- The eval suite (refusal-accuracy, groundedness, answer-quality,
  injection-resistance) runs in CI and gates ships. The build fails on a single
  strategy leak.

### 10. Edge Cases & Constraints
- **Disguised strategy intent** (jailbreak, roleplay, "for research"): the
  classifier refuses regardless of framing.
- **Account question with no retrievable data:** open a ticket, never invent.
- **Sparse KB or no relevant chunk:** escalate rather than guess.
- **PII in a draft response:** redacted before send.
- **Constraints:** ship post-launch only (launch approval required). Cost-capped
  and rate- and token-limited so it is cheap while idle. Compliance re-verified
  before the UI ships (Apple 5.1.1, Google Play generative-AI policy, and a Data
  Safety label update for the new support_conversations collection).

### 11. Risks & Mitigations

| Risk | Mitigation |
| --- | --- |
| Strategy drift | Refusal classifier plus eval gate plus production sampling |
| Hallucination | RAG grounding plus groundedness eval plus "open a ticket" over inventing |
| Scope creep (the build can balloon) | Every sprint ships standalone. S5 and S6 are cuttable |
| Over-built for current volume | Acknowledged. Justified by safety plus feedback value. Cost-capped while idle |
| Cost runaway | gpt-4o-mini plus semantic cache plus rate and token caps plus alerts |
| Review delay | Ship post-launch only |

## Prioritization

<TLDR>Chose agentic RAG with guardrails and evals over a simpler chatbot, because only a system that enforces the safety line by construction could be trusted.</TLDR>

I considered four options:

- **Expand static FAQ and help docs.** The questions were already answerable, so
  this is a findability problem, not a content gap, and it can't do account
  lookups.
- **Human support.** Doesn't scale, and routes every question through a founder,
  the exact bottleneck I was trying to remove.
- **A simple scripted or FAQ chatbot.** Hallucinates on account and billing, can't
  do live lookups, and could drift into strategy. It fails the one safety
  constraint that matters.
- **Agentic RAG with guardrails and evals (chosen).** Enforces the safety line by
  construction, grounds account answers in real tool calls, and turns support into
  a measurable product-feedback instrument.

**Owned over-investment:** this system deliberately exceeds day-1 support volume. I
am not claiming it was minimally right-sized. The over-investment is justified by a
non-negotiable safety constraint, the need to ground account answers in real data,
and a deliberate platform bet, and the cost is capped so it stays cheap while idle.

## Build & sprint summary

<TLDR>Built eval-first across six shippable sprints: input and output guardrails, RAG over pgvector, agentic tools, a ticket queue, and per-turn observability.</TLDR>

I built it as six independently shippable sprints (spec and evals, RAG layer, chat
core, chat UI, ticketing, observability and hardening), each gated by the eval
suite before merge. The architecture is an orchestrator wrapped by guardrails on
both sides:

- **Guardrails, input.** A strategy-intent and jailbreak classifier runs before
  generation. Strategy questions never reach the answer path, they get a scoped
  refusal. The system prompt is only a backstop.
- **RAG over pgvector.** The knowledge base plus the in-app glossary is chunked,
  embedded, and stored in Supabase pgvector. Retrieval is semantic top-k plus a
  re-rank, cited in the answer. Folding the 56-term glossary into the index took
  retrieval-eval coverage from 77% to 100%.
- **Agentic tools.** Auth-scoped, read-only tools let the bot act: check a real
  subscription or account, look up a term, read freemium limits. The only write
  tool is create_ticket. Tool identity comes from the session, never a model
  argument, so there is no IDOR surface.
- **Guardrails, output.** A groundedness check and PII redaction run before send.
  Unsupported claims are replaced with "let me open a ticket."
- **Ticketing.** Anything unresolved becomes a ticket with the full conversation
  and a drafted first reply, an admin queue, and Resend email. A human approves in
  seconds instead of writing from scratch.
- **Observability.** Every turn writes a trace (retrieval hits, tool calls, tokens,
  cost, latency, outcome) to a metrics dashboard, so deflection, cost, and
  groundedness are provable, not guessed.

### Retro & lessons

The lesson I keep: when one behavior is non-negotiable, enforce it with
architecture and evals, not a prompt. A strategy-intent classifier that runs before
generation, plus a CI gate that fails the build on a single leak, is what lets me
claim "0 strategy-coaching responses" and mean it. The same discipline, ground
every claim and trace every turn, is what turns an LLM demo into a system you can
actually operate.
