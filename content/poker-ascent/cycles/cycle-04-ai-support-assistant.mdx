## Summary (BLUF)

Beta testers kept asking support questions that were already answerable, routing
avoidable load through us. And some questions, if answered, would have been actively
harmful: poker-strategy coaching that competes with our own paid product. The
minimum product fix was deflection. I deliberately built more than the minimum: a
production-grade, eval-gated agentic RAG support assistant with safety guardrails,
observability, and a ticketing workflow. **I'm presenting this as an owned
over-investment, not as right-sizing.** The plan said so out loud ("this exceeds
day-1 support needs"), and the bet was justified by a hard safety constraint plus
the system's most valuable byproduct. Provable-now results: **0 strategy-coaching
leaks across 1,000 adversarial prompts**, **60/60 grounded answers**, and a
**clustered map of what actually confuses users**. Deflection and CSAT are still
stabilizing as post-launch volume grows.

## Trigger & problem

<TLDR>The hard part wasn't deflecting easy questions, it was making it structurally impossible for a poker-training bot to coach strategy or invent account facts.</TLDR>

Support load split into three kinds of message with very different correct handling:
routine ("how do I cancel?", resolve from the knowledge base), account-specific
("did my subscription renew?", which needs a live, accurate lookup), and
out-of-scope-and-dangerous ("how should I play AK on the button?"). That third kind
is the spine of the problem: coaching strategy would make the bot a poker authority,
**cannibalize the paid trainer, and contradict the app's own engine.** So the
requirement wasn't just "deflect." It was "deflect while making it structurally
impossible to drift into strategy coaching or to invent facts about someone's
account."

## Discovery & user-research synthesis

<TLDR>The sharper signal: many easy questions are symptoms, not support gaps, so support load is really a diagnostic surface for product problems.</TLDR>

The dominant beta signal was the volume of easily-answerable questions. But the
sharper observation: **many of those questions are symptoms, not support gaps.** If
users repeatedly ask "where's my streak," the real fix is the product, not a better
FAQ answer. That reframed support load as a *diagnostic surface*, which later drove
the most valuable metric in this cycle.

## The scoping tension (owned, not hidden)

<TLDR>I knowingly over-built for day-1 volume, and I'm naming it, because the over-investment is defensible only if it's owned, tied to a real safety constraint, and cost-capped.</TLDR>

The honest minimum to handle easily-answerable questions is far less than what I
built. I am naming that directly, because unacknowledged over-engineering reads as
poor judgment, and the right-sizing instinct matters elsewhere in this portfolio.

I over-invested deliberately, for reasons I can defend:

1. **The safety line is non-negotiable and needs real enforcement.** "Never coach
   strategy" can't rest on a system-prompt instruction, because prompts leak. It
   needs a pre-generation intent classifier plus an eval gate that fails the build
   on a single leak. That is not gold-plating. It is the only way to make the
   harmful behavior structurally impossible.
2. **Account-specific answers must be grounded.** Telling a user the wrong thing
   about their own billing is worse than not answering, so a groundedness check that
   replaces unsupported claims with "let me open a ticket" is a real requirement,
   not a flourish.
3. **A platform and skill bet.** Evals, observability, and tool-use are table stakes
   for a serious LLM system, and building them now was an intentional investment.

The judgment I'm claiming isn't "I built the minimum." It is this: **I knew I was
over-building for day-1 volume, I said so in the plan, I capped cost so it's cheap
while idle, and the honest risk is that if volume never grows, this is expensive
infrastructure for a small problem.**

## Prioritization

<TLDR>Chose agentic RAG with guardrails and evals, knowingly over-built, because a naive bot fails the one safety constraint that matters.</TLDR>

- **Expand static FAQ and help docs.** Cheapest, but the questions were already
  answerable, so this is a findability problem, not a content gap. More docs users
  don't read doesn't solve it, and it can't do account-specific lookups.
- **Human support.** Doesn't scale, and routes every question through a founder, the
  exact bottleneck pattern this portfolio keeps removing.
- **Simple scripted or FAQ chatbot.** Cheap, but it hallucinates on account and
  billing, can't do live lookups, and, critically, could drift into answering poker
  strategy. A naive bot fails the one safety constraint that matters.
- **Agentic RAG with guardrails and evals (chosen, knowingly over-built).** The only
  option that enforces the safety line by construction, grounds account answers in
  real tool calls, and turns support into a measurable feedback instrument.

## Build & sprint summary

<TLDR>An eval-first agentic orchestrator wrapped by input and output guardrails, grounded by RAG and auth-scoped tools, escalating to a drafted ticket, shipped sprint by sprint post-launch.</TLDR>

A grounded orchestrator (OpenAI gpt-4o-mini for chat and tool-use) wrapped by input
and output guardrails (a strategy-intent classifier, a groundedness check, and PII
redaction), fed by RAG over pgvector and auth-scoped read-only tools, with every
turn traced, and unresolved sessions escalated into a ticket with a bot-drafted
first reply. Built eval-first, shipped sprint by sprint, post-launch only.

The chat and classifier run on gpt-4o-mini. The system was originally specced on
Claude Haiku, and a thin provider abstraction made the switch to one-provider
billing a localized change.

### 1. Summary
A production-grade, agentic RAG support assistant: a grounded AI that resolves most
support queries autonomously, escalates the rest into a ticketing workflow with a
bot-drafted first reply, and is built eval-first with full observability. It is safe
by construction, it does support, never poker-strategy coaching. The hard
requirement: maximize deflection while making it structurally impossible for the
assistant to drift into strategy coaching or to hallucinate facts about a user's
account or billing.

### 2. Problem & Background
Support load splits into three kinds of message with very different correct
handling: routine (resolve from the knowledge base), account-specific (resolve via a
live, auth-scoped account lookup), and out-of-scope or dangerous (refuse, because
coaching strategy makes the bot a poker authority that competes with the paid
product and contradicts the engine). Beta testers generated a high volume of the
first kind, while the third kind defines the product's spine.

### 3. Discovery
The dominant beta signal was the volume of easily-answerable questions. The sharper
observation: many of these are symptoms of product confusion, not support-content
gaps. Repeated "where's my streak" or "charged twice" questions point at product
issues, not missing FAQ entries. This reframed support load as a diagnostic surface
feeding the knowledge base and product fixes.

### 4. Goals & Non-Goals
**Goals**

- Deflect about 70% of support sessions.
- Ground every factual claim in retrieved content or a live tool call.
- Escalate cleanly, with full context and a drafted first reply.

**Safety goal (gating)**

- Zero strategy-coaching responses in production.

**Non-Goals**

- General poker Q&A, judging drill correctness, anything that competes with the
  trainer.

### 5. Success Metrics

| Metric | Type | Target | Result so far |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Strategy-coaching leaks | Safety (gating) | 0 in prod | 0 / 1,000 adversarial prompts (CI-gated) |
| Groundedness | Guardrail | at least 95% | 60 / 60 grounded |
| Confusion map | Diagnostic | coherent clusters | billing and streak product signals surfaced |
| Deflection rate | Primary | about 70% | early, stabilizing as volume grows |
| CSAT (thumbs) | Health | at least 80% | early, accumulating |
| Cost / resolution | Health | under cap | about $0.0005 per message |

### 6. Solution Overview
An orchestrator (gpt-4o-mini) wrapped on both sides by guardrails, fed by RAG and
live, auth-scoped tools, with every turn traced. Core stances: grounding over
recall, refusal as a first-class path (a classifier runs before generation),
agentic but read-only (the only write tool is create_ticket), and eval-first (the
safety metric is a hard CI gate).

### 7. Functional Requirements
- Input guardrail classifies intent. Out-of-scope or strategy goes to a scoped
  refusal.
- Allowed messages retrieve top-k KB chunks (re-ranked, cited).
- Account intents trigger auth-scoped, read-only tool calls.
- Output guardrail enforces groundedness and PII redaction before send.
- Unresolved sessions call create_ticket with the conversation and a drafted reply.
- Every turn emits a trace. The eval suite runs in CI and fails the build on a
  single strategy leak.

### 8. Edge Cases & Constraints
- Disguised strategy intent (jailbreak, roleplay, "for research"): refused
  regardless of framing.
- Account question with no retrievable data: open a ticket, never invent.
- Constraints: ship post-launch only. Cost-capped and rate-limited so it is cheap
  while idle.

### 9. Alternatives Considered

| Option | Why not |
| --- | --- |
| Expand static FAQ / help docs | Questions were already answerable, a findability problem, and it can't do account lookups. |
| Human support | Doesn't scale, routes every question through a founder. |
| Simple scripted / FAQ chatbot | Hallucinates on account and billing, and could drift into strategy. Fails the one safety constraint that matters. |
| Agentic RAG with guardrails and evals (chosen) | Enforces the safety line by construction, grounds answers in real tool calls, and turns support into a product-feedback instrument. |

Ordered by what is provable now, because this is a recent project, and the honest
move is to lead with the eval-readable results and let the volume-dependent ones be
openly early.

**1. Safety, the headline.** 0 strategy-coaching leaks across 1,000 adversarial
prompts (15 jailbreak techniques: DAN-mode, roleplay, fake-developer, "ignore
instructions," emotional framing, "for research," one-word, system-prompt
extraction, and more). 100% refused. Enforced as a CI gate, the build fails on a
single leak. Honest caveat: this is a synthetic adversarial suite, so the claim is
"0 leaks across a 1,000-prompt red-team, gated in CI," not "unjailbreakable." Live
users are weirder than any test set, and that is part of what is still accumulating.
The mechanism (gate on a single leak) is as much the result as the number.

**2. The confusion map, the metric that justifies the build.** Incoming questions
are embedded and clustered into "what are users confused about." The clusters were
coherent and actionable, and two of them aren't bot-tuning signals at all, they are
product signals the bot surfaced:

- a **billing cluster** (charged twice, billed again, charged after cancelling), a
  possible billing or cancellation-flow problem.
- a **streak cluster** (where's my streak, why did it reset, it disappeared), a
  streak-logic or visibility problem.

This is the reframe that makes the over-engineering pay off: **the bot's most
valuable first-week output wasn't deflection, it was a ranked map of product
problems to fix upstream so the questions stop being asked.** Support load became a
product-feedback engine. Honest caveat: the clustering input mixed real beta
questions with synthetic ones, so the next step is to filter to real-only and
confirm whether the billing and streak clusters are live user incidents. If they
are, "N real users independently reported double-charging" becomes a concrete
product finding worth stating outright.

**3. Groundedness.** 60 of 60 answered responses grounded in retrieved KB
(LLM-as-judge). An earlier flagged miss (from a smaller 22-prompt run) was
human-reviewed and found to be a judge false-positive: the answer was grounded, the
judge erred. We treat judge output as auditable, not ground truth. Honest note: 60
is a controlled eval set, so a clean score reflects the suite, not a permanent
property. Live groundedness is tracked as volume grows.

**4. Operational, openly early.** Deflection rate (target about 70%), CSAT (thumbs,
target 80% or more), cost per resolution, and p50 TTFT are instrumented and
accumulating, but post-launch volume is still small, so these are directional only
and tracked over time, not reported as settled results.

### Retro & lessons

- The most valuable thing a support bot produced wasn't answers, it was a
  prioritized list of what confuses users. Build support as an *instrument*, not
  just a deflector.
- Owned over-engineering is defensible, unowned over-engineering isn't. The
  discipline was naming the over-investment, tying it to a real safety constraint,
  and capping cost, not pretending it was minimal.
- This is the same bottleneck-removal instinct as the rest of the portfolio
  (support previously routed through a human), applied to a safety-critical surface
  where the guardrails, not the deflection, are the point.
